## LinkedIn Posts (2 posts)

### LinkedIn Post 1 — Founder Tone

**Why we built ThumbnailOS**

There are thousands of Indian creators making genuinely great content — finance explainers in Hindi, cooking channels in Tamil, tech breakdowns in regional languages — that are stuck at 10K–30K subscribers despite posting consistently for years.

I kept asking: why aren't these channels growing faster?

The content is solid. The audience is there. But the thumbnails? Most of them are doing one or more things that directly suppress click-through rate: no face, cluttered text, low contrast, no emotional hook.

We spent the last few weeks reviewing 100 channels in detail. The pattern was unmistakable.

So we built ThumbnailOS — a structured audit service that tells creators exactly what's wrong with their thumbnails and what to change. The first 50 audits are free.

If you're a creator, or know one who's been grinding without the growth to match — this is for them.

---

### LinkedIn Post 2 — Data/Insight Tone

**Indian regional language YouTube is underserved. The thumbnail problem is part of why.**

We reviewed 100 Indian YouTube channels across Hindi, Tamil, Marathi, and Assamese content niches. The data told a consistent story:

- 84% had at least 2 critical thumbnail mistakes
- Channels with cluttered text thumbnails averaged 30–40% lower estimated CTR vs. competitors in the same niche
- Almost none were using facial expressions or emotional cues — the highest-performing CTR signal on YouTube

These creators aren't lazy. They're just not getting the thumbnail feedback loop that English-language creators get from communities like r/youtubers or YouTube creator academies. That gap is what ThumbnailOS is trying to close.

We're offering free audits to the first 50 creators who apply. No strings. Just a structured review of what's hurting your CTR and a clear fix.

If this resonates with someone you know, send it their way.

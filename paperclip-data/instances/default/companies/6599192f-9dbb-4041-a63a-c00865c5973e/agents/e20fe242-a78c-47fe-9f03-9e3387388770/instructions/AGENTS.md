## Best Practices

1. **No brief, no copy.** Always request a creative brief before starting any copy project. It forces clarity on audience, message, funnel stage, and success metric.
2. **Mine voice-of-customer language.** Steal exact phrases from product reviews, Reddit threads, and UGC comments. Your audience's words outconvert your marketing words every time.
3. **Mobile-first review.** Write and review every piece on a 375px screen. Over 80% of ad and landing page traffic is mobile.
4. **One goal per piece.** Every page, email, and ad should drive a single action. Multiple CTAs dilute intent.
5. **Specificity = credibility.** Replace every vague claim with a specific number, timeframe, or example. "72% saw results in 14 days" > "most users see results quickly."
6. **Test the headline first.** The H1 has the highest leverage of any copy element. Run A/B tests on headlines before anything else.
7. **Message match is mandatory.** Ad copy and landing page headline must mirror the same promise. Mismatched messaging can halve conversion rates.
8. **Document every A/B test result.** Wins and losses go into the copy learnings library. The loser's data builds the next winner.
9. **Refresh copy regularly.** Audit high-traffic product pages and landing pages quarterly. Stale copy = invisible copy.
10. **Read the style guide before every project.** Brand voice consistency compounds into brand equity. One off-tone email can undermine months of brand building.

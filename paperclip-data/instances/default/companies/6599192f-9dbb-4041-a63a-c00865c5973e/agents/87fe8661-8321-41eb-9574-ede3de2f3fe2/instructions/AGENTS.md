## Best Practices

1. **Creative is the primary performance lever** — 70-80% of ad performance comes from creative quality, not audience or bid strategy. Maintain creative velocity: 5-10 new concepts/week per active channel.

2. **Server-side tracking is non-negotiable** — Deploy both pixel AND server-side (Meta CAPI, Google Enhanced Conversions). Garbage data input equals garbage Smart Bidding output.

3. **Separate Test and Scale campaigns** — Never test new creatives inside high-budget production campaigns. A separate test campaign (10-15% of budget) preserves learning phases for scale campaigns.

4. **PMax and Standard Shopping are complementary, not competing** — Use Standard Shopping for high-value SKU control; use Performance Max for reach, discovery, and multi-inventory coverage.

5. **Budget rule: 70/20/10** — 70% to proven performers, 20% to optimization of existing, 10% to new experiments and platforms.

6. **Exclusions matter as much as inclusions** — Exclude recent purchasers from acquisition, suppress churned users, exclude branded traffic from non-brand campaigns.

7. **Attribution informs strategy, not day-to-day decisions** — Use a multi-touch or data-driven model for strategic channel allocation; use platform-native reporting for day-to-day optimization.

8. **First-party data is your competitive moat** — Upload customer lists to Meta and Google regularly. Seed lookalikes from top 20% LTV customers only.

9. **Change one thing at a time** — Structural changes to PMax reset learning periods. Hold to a 2-week cadence for PMax structural changes; budget adjustments above 20% in a single step also trigger resets.

10. **Escalate fast on performance drops** — A >30% ROAS decline week-over-week or any account suspension requires immediate escalation to CMO, not waiting for the next report.

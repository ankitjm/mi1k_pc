# MILK — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

## Overview

This document defines who Milk sells to, how to qualify prospects, and how to navigate the buying process. Every agent involved in sales, marketing, or client communication should reference this.

---

## Primary ICP

### Company Profile

| Attribute | Ideal Fit |
|-----------|-----------|
| Company size | 20–200 employees |
| Revenue | ₹5Cr–₹200Cr+ / $500K–$25M+ |
| Stage | Growth-stage, scaling operations |
| Industries | Real Estate, D2C / E-commerce (primary); professional services, SaaS, media (secondary) |
| Geography | India-first, with global adaptability |
| AI maturity | Curious but not yet implemented, or early experiments that haven't scaled |
| Operational pain | Execution bottlenecks, manual repetitive workflows, team bandwidth constraints |

### What Makes Them a Fit

- They have clear business processes that are currently manual or semi-manual
- Their team is stretched thin — not because of poor hiring, but because execution demands outpace headcount
- They've tried tools (Zapier, Make, ChatGPT, etc.) but couldn't build a cohesive system
- Leadership is open to AI but doesn't have internal capacity to build and manage AI systems
- They value speed and outcomes over lengthy evaluation cycles

### What Makes Them a Bad Fit

- No defined workflows (still figuring out what their processes even are)
- Looking for a chatbot or a single-use AI tool
- Want to build everything in-house and just need advice
- Extremely risk-averse or require 6+ month evaluation cycles
- Budget for only a one-time project with no interest in ongoing execution

## Buyer Personas

### Persona 1: Growth-Stage Founder / CEO

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Title | Founder, CEO, Managing Director |
| Motivation | Scale output without proportionally scaling headcount |
| Pain points | Team stretched thin, execution bottlenecks, AI curiosity but no internal capacity |
| Decision style | Fast, vision-driven, wants proof-of-concept quickly |
| Budget authority | Full |
| Key question | "Can this actually run our workflows, or is it another tool we have to manage?" |
| What wins them | A live demo showing a real workflow running end-to-end |
| What loses them | Jargon, long timelines, vague outcomes |

### Persona 2: Head of Marketing / Sales

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Title | VP Marketing, Head of Sales, Growth Lead, Revenue Head |
| Motivation | Hit targets with limited bandwidth |
| Pain points | Too many manual tasks, slow campaign/proposal turnaround, creative bottlenecks |
| Decision style | ROI-driven, needs to justify spend to leadership |
| Budget authority | Partial — can champion internally, may need CEO sign-off |
| Key question | "How quickly can this start producing output I can actually use?" |
| What wins them | Specific examples of output (a campaign brief generated in 2 minutes, a proposal drafted from a prompt) |
| What loses them | Abstract capability pitches, no clear timeline to value |

### Persona 3: Technical Decision-Maker (CTO / VP Engineering)

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Title | CTO, VP Engineering, Head of Product, Head of Technology |
| Motivation | Implement AI without creating tech debt or vendor lock-in |
| Pain points | Skeptical of AI hype, pressure to adopt AI, needs clean integrations |
| Decision style | Evidence-based, architecture-aware, integration-focused |
| Budget authority | Partial — influences technical feasibility, may not own business budget |
| Key question | "How does this plug into our existing stack without creating tech debt?" |
| What wins them | Transparent architecture, clean API integrations, no black boxes |
| What loses them | Proprietary lock-in, inability to explain how the system works |

## Qualification Framework

### BANT+ Criteria

| Criteria | Qualifying Signal |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Budget** | Has allocated or can allocate ₹1L–₹10L+/month ($1K–$12K+) for execution improvement |
| **Authority** | Speaking with founder/CEO, or a champion with direct access to budget holder |
| **Need** | Can name specific workflows that are manual, slow, or breaking at scale |
| **Timeline** | Wants to start within 2–6 weeks, not 6 months |
| **Fit** | Has repeatable workflows, uses digital tools, team of 20+ |

### Disqualification Signals

- "We just want a chatbot for our website" → Not a fit
- "We need to evaluate this for the next two quarters" → Too slow
- "Can you just give us a recommendation?" → They want consulting, not execution enablement
- "We don't really have defined processes" → Not ready for Milk
- "Our budget is under ₹50K/month" → Below threshold for meaningful engagement

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We can build this ourselves" | "You absolutely can — the question is whether that's the best use of your engineering team's time. Milk gets you to a running system in weeks, not quarters, so your team can focus on your core product." |
| "How is this different from Zapier/Make?" | "Those are tools. You still need to design the workflow, build the automations, maintain them, and fix them when they break. Milk does all of that — we're the team that builds and runs the system, not just the tool." |
| "AI isn't reliable enough yet" | "That's exactly why you need structured workflows, not just raw AI. Milk builds systems with human checkpoints, fallback logic, and quality controls. The AI does the heavy lifting; the system keeps it in check." |
| "We've tried AI tools and they didn't work" | "Most AI tools fail because they're disconnected from your actual workflows. Milk starts with your business intent and builds the execution layer around it — not the other way around." |
| "This seems expensive" | "What's the cost of your team spending 20 hours a week on work that could be systemized? Milk typically pays for itself within the first month through time saved alone." |

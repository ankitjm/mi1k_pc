## Post 5 — Tuesday, Week 3
**Theme: The data blindspot no distributor talks about**

Your brand rep visits 12 retailers in a day.

He carries a notepad. He calls you in the evening. He says "most of them seem okay on stock."

You have no idea what that means.

- Okay as in 3 units? 30 units?
- Which SKUs moved? Which ones didn't?
- Which retailer needs a push before month-end?

The problem isn't your field team. They're doing their job.

The problem is there's no system capturing what they see.

At Khosha Systems, we saw this exact gap with a client who had 80+ retailers across 3 cities. Their field team visited regularly. But at HQ, nobody could answer: "What's on the shelves right now?"

So we built something simple. A mobile form their sales team fills in after every visit. Takes 2 minutes. Feeds into a live dashboard.

First week of using it, the brand discovered 2 retailers had been sitting on dead stock for 6 months. That stock was never going to sell itself.

You can't fix what you can't see.

What does your field team capture after a retailer visit today?

## Post 7 — Tuesday, Week 4
**Theme: Why electronics retailers don't trust their own numbers**

I've heard this line from 3 different retailers in 3 different cities:

"Mera system mein alag dikhta hai, actual mein alag hota hai."
(My system shows one thing. Reality is different.)

The gap between system stock and physical stock is one of the least talked-about problems in electronics distribution.

How does it happen?

- Barcode scan missed at billing
- Return entered incorrectly
- Staff manually adjusted stock "to match" physical count
- Demo unit counted as saleable stock

Every adjustment feels small. But after 6 months, you're looking at a ₹4–8 lakh discrepancy on a single product category.

The fix isn't always technology. Sometimes it's a 15-minute physical count every Monday, captured in a simple form, compared against your billing software.

We helped a Bengaluru distributor cut this discrepancy by 70% in 60 days. No ERP upgrade. No retraining. Just a consistent weekly process.

Consistency beats complexity.

Is your stock count system telling you the truth?

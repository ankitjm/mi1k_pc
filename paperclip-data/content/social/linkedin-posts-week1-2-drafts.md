## Post 1 — Tuesday, Week 1
**Theme: The ₹2–3 lakh/month problem no electronics retailer talks about**

Most electronics retailers don't know they're bleeding money.

Not because they're careless. Because their billing software doesn't track the right thing.

Here's the problem:

A retailer sells 10 Samsung Galaxy A35 units. Their system shows: Samsung A35 — qty 10. Done.

But which 10? Which serial numbers? Which ones came from which batch? Which ones are under warranty?

They don't know.

Three months later, a unit comes back for exchange. Was it actually sold from their shop, or is someone returning a unit bought elsewhere? Without IMEI tracking, there's no way to confirm.

This gap — between SKU-level and IMEI-level tracking — costs the average mid-sized electronics retailer ₹2–3 lakh per month. In wrong returns, warranty fraud, and misallocated stock.

The fix isn't complicated. It's a single toggle in how billing is done.

We built IMEI-level tracking into RetailerOS because we kept hearing the same story from retailers across Bangalore and Hyderabad. "Pata hi nahi tha yeh itna bada problem tha."

(We didn't know this was such a big problem.)

Now they do.

Does your billing software track serial numbers at the point of sale?

## Post 4 — Thursday, Week 2
**Theme: Stop calling Tally a POS system. It's not.**

This might ruffle some feathers.

Tally is excellent accounting software. It is not a point-of-sale system.

The difference matters — especially for electronics retailers.

A POS system should:
- Capture IMEI/serial numbers at billing
- Track brand schemes and auto-apply applicable discounts
- Flag low-stock items before they run out
- Generate GST-compliant invoices in under 30 seconds
- Integrate with your distributor's catalog in real time

Tally does none of these things natively.

Yet thousands of electronics retailers in India run their entire sales operation through Tally because "that's what we've always used."

The result? Manual serial number registers in notebooks. Scheme calculations done on paper. Stock reports that are always 48 hours behind. GST corrections eating 6–8 hours a week.

I'm not saying replace Tally for accounting. It's great for that.

I'm saying: your billing workflow deserves purpose-built tools.

Tally for books. RetailerOS for the shop floor. Each doing what it was actually built for.

What software does your billing run on today?
